Hotels and Homelessness in New York

According to Coalition for the Homeless, homelessness in New York City has reached the highest levels since the Great Depression of the 1930s.

April, 2020

At the peak of the pandemic, De Blasio's administration signed a $300 million contract with the hotel industry to temporarily house 10 thousand homeless people around the city.

June, 2021

DeBlasio orders it was time for homeless people to go back to their respective shelters, adding that the city was “recovering”.

Edwin Martinez is one of the few homeless still living in hotels.

Martinez says his experiencie living in a shelter was "worse than jail".

The NY Doctors Coalition emphasized that moving people to bunk-style shelters "directly threatens the health of thousands of homeless new yorkers, and indirectly that of the entire city". The order by De Blasio ran counter to CDC guidelines.

This move by the city puts thousands of homeless people at risk. 42% of the city’s total homeless population remains unvaccinated, according to Health Department data.

Artel Hotel in Bushwick, Brooklyn where many homeless people lived. It stands empty now.

Jacquelyn Simone, Policy Adviser at Coalition of for the Homeless emphasized “the hotel program should not have ended while the virus continues to spread throughout the city".
